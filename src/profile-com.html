Position Profile

Operations Manager

A year-round leadership seat that works hand-in-hand with every Area Manager — directing operations across Brite Nites and Brite Labs, residential and commercial, building and directing training, and developing the field-management bench. The bridge between the company’s growth ambitions and the work that gets done in the field.

TypeYear-round · leadership
Reports toDirector of Operations
OwnsOperations · Training
SpansBrite Nites · Brite Labs
Region focusAZ (today) → national

Purpose of the role

Builds the capabilities the operation runs on — trained people, a deeper field-management bench, and commercial delivery.

The Operations Manager runs operations across the business — embedded day-to-day with every Area Manager, owning the training function, and developing the Area Manager bench so the field can scale. The scope spans both Brite Nites and Brite Labs, residential and commercial work alike. Where the Director of Operations runs the core operation, this seat builds the next layer of capability beneath it — the people and programs that staff the field, and the delivery of the company’s commercial jobs, where this seat carries particular weight.

Today the seat also directs operations for the AZ market and provides mobile support to Area Managers across regions — stepping in on the road wherever a market or seat needs reinforcement.

The title reflects today’s broad mandate. As the Area Manager bench is built out and the commercial footprint grows nationally, the seat is expected to concentrate on commercial operations — with the title specializing toward Commercial Operations as the business does.

Reporting & mandate

Where it sits

  • Reports to the Director of Operations.
  • Works alongside the Regional Dispatch Coordinators and is a senior resource for Area Managers — escalation, coaching, and the partner on larger commercial jobs.
  • Coordinates with the Sales team on commercial pipeline and delivery.

Scope today vs. end-state

  • Today: works across all Area Managers + training + commercial jobs + AZ operations + mobile AM support — spanning Brite Nites and Brite Labs.
  • End-state: national commercial operations leader + owner of training.
  • The title is set to today’s broad scope and is expected to specialize toward Commercial Operations (and a Director/VP of Commercial Operations) as the bench is built and the commercial footprint goes national.

Core responsibilities

  • Operations across the Area Managers — embedded with every AM as the cross-territory operations and standards resource, spanning Brite Nites and Brite Labs, residential and commercial.
  • Commercial operations — owns delivery of commercial jobs: scoping, coordination, and partnering directly with Area Managers on the larger, more complex installs.
  • Training — builds and directs the training program across every operational seat: curriculum, role tracks, and the standards crews are held to.
  • Bench development — grows the Area Manager pipeline so territories can be staffed with developed AMs rather than over-stretched stopgaps.
  • AZ market operations — directs operations for the Arizona market.
  • Mobile field support — provides on-the-road reinforcement to Area Managers across regions where a market or seat needs help.
  • Commercial growth — builds the operational foundation to run commercial work at national scale as the footprint expands.

Who the role works with

Director of Operations
The seat this role reports to; owns core operations company-wide.
Regional Dispatch Coordinator (RDC)
Runs the dispatch board across a cluster of territories; a peer-level operational counterpart this seat supports and escalates with.
Area Managers (AM)

Sales
The team that books commercial work; this seat coordinates pipeline and delivery with them.
