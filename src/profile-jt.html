Junior Tech (JT)

The entry-level field installer. A JT does the hands-on install work on a Senior Tech's crew, attached to that crew for the day — which is how Brite Nites captures labor cost per job.

Purpose of the role

The JT is the hands on the install — entry-level labor that executes the design under the crew leader's direction, one jobsite at a time.

Junior Tech is the highest-volume seasonal hire at Brite Nites and the front door to the field. No prior experience is assumed; the role is built to be trained from scratch through a phased program before a JT is cleared to work live sites. The work itself varies day to day — rooflines one day, a full day in a tree the next, a mix of products the day after — but the daily process and the standards never change.

A JT always works on a Senior Tech's crew, attached to that crew for the day. That attachment is also the company's unit of cost capture: a JT's day on a crew is how labor cost gets booked against the jobs that crew completes. The JT's job is to execute clean, accurate, safe install work so the crew finishes to standard and the install survives the season's quality checks and strike audit.

The role is deliberately grounded: task execution, not decision-making. The ST owns the job from arrival to client sign-off and directs the work; the JT does the work well, moves to the next task without being told, and grows toward the Senior Tech seat.

Reporting & compensation

Where it sits

  • Day-to-day boss: the Senior Tech (ST) leading the crew the JT is attached to that day.
  • Crew size: 2–4 people, working residential and a growing commercial book.
  • Training & gate decisions: owned by the Area Manager (AM), the in-field boss for the territory.
  • Territory: sits inside the AM's territory (or a Territory Manager's stretched area where one is in place).
  • No direct reports. The JT is the bottom rung of the field crew.

Handbook note: The current handbook titles this seat Junior Field Technician and routes it up through the Senior Field Technician and Area Manager to a Territory Manager (a separate seat). This profile uses the redesigned naming and treats the Territory Manager as an Area Manager stretched over more ground.

Compensation

  • Seasonal field position — hired for Peak/Install season and may continue through strike, with a winter break between the two.
  • Paid as entry-level field labor; the JT's day on a crew is how labor cost is captured per job.
  • Unlike the ST, the JT does not earn a production-pay share of installed revenue — that lever belongs to the crew leader.
  • Continuation & advancement depend on sustained performance: showing up on time, clean work to standard, safety discipline, and speed.
  • Specific pay figures aren't set here — see the JT learning track / offer for current numbers.

Core responsibilities

  • Show up on time and clock in via the Deputy app — a watched, non-negotiable.
  • Prep the truck: clean the cab and bed, clear all trash and leftover product from the prior day.
  • Load the day's material per the manifest in the Crew App — no more than specified.
  • Walk the job with the ST first. Never start installing before the crew leader has assigned what goes where; stay in the assigned zone.
  • Pull only what's needed from the truck — ladders, strands, materials. If more strands than the manifest are needed, ask the ST and track the total to avoid over-installation.
  • Execute the install across the full product range — rooflines (C9/C7 bulbs), icicles, stake lights, LED mini strands (tree wraps, gates, bushes, columns, willow lights), spheres, wreaths, bows, garland, and digital timers.
  • Work carefully around property: client trees can be high-value; if a branch breaks, stop, pick it up, dispose of it, document. Photograph any damaged plant bed and route it to the manager — never freelance the client comm.
  • Clean up every site before leaving: walk the property for trash, tools, and dropped staples; tie down ladders and tuck the slack before driving off.
  • Hold install etiquette: uniform always, no profanity or loud talk at customer homes, no smoking on site, headphones only with one ear out, and no promising return visits or service dates.

Two daily non-negotiables run through everything: show up on time, and leave every site clean. Both are watched and both affect strike work and continuation.

Who the role works with

Senior Tech (ST) — crew leader
The JT's day-to-day boss. Owns the job from arrival to client sign-off, directs the JT's work, and decides when more material is pulled. The JT executes; the ST leads.
Area Manager (AM)
The in-field boss for the territory. Owns the JT's training and gate/clearance decisions, runs early-season quality checks, and makes the damage/negligence calls.
RDC (Regional Dispatch Coordinator)
The JT's channel for back-office questions — hours, pay, and HR all route to the RDC. Field and install direction stays with the ST and AM; the RDC handles the administrative side.
Territory Manager (TM)
Where present, the same in-field seat stretched over a larger area; handles client communication on issues like plant or tree damage the JT documents.
Quality Pro (QP)
The inspector who verifies installs were completed to the design accurately. The JT's work is what QP checks and scores — clean, accurate work keeps the crew's ranking up.
Warehouse (WA)
Stages and pulls the next day's product against the schedule. The JT loads from what the warehouse has prepped and returns the truck clean.
